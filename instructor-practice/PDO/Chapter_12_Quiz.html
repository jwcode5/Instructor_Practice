Pumper Driver/Operator Chapter 12 Practice

1. The pumper that takes water from a hydrant or static source at the beginning of a relay operation is the:





Correct answer: D

2. The pumper that receives water from the source pumper, raises the pressure, and then supplies water to the next apparatus is the:





Correct answer: B

3. The factor that MOST determines the need for relay operations is:





Correct answer: D

4. Which statement about pressure during relay pumping operations for the attack pumper is MOST accurate?





Correct answer: C

5. Which should be the first apparatus discontinued when relay operations are no longer needed?





Correct answer: A

6. Which relay method requires no adapters or other appliances to connect intake hoses in the system?





Correct answer: A

7. Which valve opens and dumps excess pressure/water until water is entering pump at correct pressure?





Correct answer: C

8. Which valve is placed along the length of a supply hose and permits a pumper to connect to the valve to boost pressure in the hose?





Correct answer: B

9. What three items directly affect friction loss in a relay system?


Correct answer: Answers should include the following:
Amount of water being flowed
Diameter of the hose
Distance between the pumpers

10. What determines the overall capacity of a relay system?


Correct answer: The smallest pump and the smallest diameter of hose used in the relay

11. Why must driver/operators remember to keep water moving through the pump when using a static water supply?


Correct answer: Because failure to do so may result in loss of prime or overheating pump which would delay operations

12. What is the function of bleeder valves?


Correct answer: Allow air to bleed off as the incoming supply hose is charged
