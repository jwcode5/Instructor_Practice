Correct answer: D

Correct answer: A

4. Which term refers to the height a column of water may be lifted in sufficient quantity to provide a reliable fire flow?





Correct answer: D

5. Access to a natural water source is MOST likely to be impeded by:





Correct answer: D

6. Which water source may be placed within strategic locations by some suburban and urban jurisdictions as a backup water supply system?





Correct answer: A

7. Which water source often has improved roads that lead to the water’s edge and may have dry hydrants installed for facility personnel or firefighters to use for water supply?





Correct answer: C

8. Which water source may have open canals running through a property that may feature several sites suitable for drafting by fire department pumpers?





Correct answer: D

9. What is the formula for net pump discharge pressure at draft?


Correct answer: NPDP Draft = PDP + PC

10. What should first be determined when evaluating a potential natural water supply source?


Correct answer: Adequacy and accessibility

11. List three artificial static water supply sources.


Correct answer: Answers may vary; students should include at least three of the following:
Cisterns
Private water storage tanks
Ground reservoirs
Swimming pools
Agricultural irrigation systems

12. When conducting preincident planning, what should be considered for swimming pools that may be used for fire protection?


Correct answer: Accessibility and capacity
